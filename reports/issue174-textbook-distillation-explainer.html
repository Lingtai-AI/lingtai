Textbook Distillation Skill

Teach yourself from any book, no instructor required. LingTai distills a textbook into a structured learning track and ships self-contained HTML lecture notes in the style you ask for — with worked examples, exercises, and checkpoints to tell you when you're ready to move on.

What it does

This adds a bundled utility skill, textbook-distillation, under tui/internal/preset/skills/. When you ask LingTai to help you study a book on your own, the agent loads this skill and runs a five-phase, human-in-the-loop workflow that turns the source into lessons you can work through at your own pace.

Distill, don't copy

Concepts are re-explained in the agent's own words with fresh examples — never a reproduction of the author's text.

Your style

Palette, layout, emphasis pattern, tone, and density are yours to specify. The notes are built for that style from the start.

Active learning

Every lesson carries worked examples, graded exercises, and a checkpoint with collapsible answers.

The workflow

1

Intake

Identify the source, confirm authorized access, capture the learning goal and style.

→
2

Chapter map

Skeleton of units, core concepts, prerequisites, weight, and source locations. You approve scope.

→
3

Lesson plan

A sequence of one-sitting lessons with objectives, examples, exercises, checkpoints. You approve granularity.

→
4

HTML notes

One self-contained styled .html per lesson, MathJax-ready, validated.

→
5

Review loop

React to lesson 1, then batch the rest plus an index page.

Phases 2 and 3 are approval gates: nothing gets generated as HTML until you've confirmed the map and the plan match your goal.

What a lesson contains

SectionPurpose
Header & provenanceTitle, objective, source citation (title/author/edition/pages), timestamp, and a caveat that these are re-explained notes — not the original.
PrerequisitesLinks back to the lessons you should have done.
Concept expositionThe teaching, in the agent's own words, with definitions boxed and math rendered via MathJax.
Worked exampleThe reasoning stepped through explicitly.
Exercises2–5 problems, easy → hard, solutions hidden behind <details> so you try first.
Checkpoint3–6 self-check questions with collapsible answers — passing them is the signal to advance.

Customizable HTML

A bundled template at assets/lesson-template.html wires up the hard parts so the agent can focus on teaching:

  • CSS variables at the top of <style> for palette, accent, and density
  • MathJax configured for \( … \) inline and \[ … \] display math
  • Definition / key-formula / warning callout boxes for a consistent emphasis hierarchy
  • Collapsible exercise solutions and checkpoint answers
  • A print stylesheet that drops navigation and expands answers
  • One file, no external assets except the MathJax CDN — open it anywhere

HTML mechanics (skeleton, MathJax rules, validation checklist) are reused from the existing swiss-knife → html-report reference rather than duplicated, so equation handling stays consistent across LingTai.

Source limits, copyright & safety

Boundaries — not optional The skill is built to help you learn from a book, not to pirate it.
  • Distill, do not reproduce — re-explain concepts; no pasted chapters, no copied prose, no recreated figures.
  • No piracy enabler — requests like "summarize the whole book so I don't have to buy it" are declined in favor of legitimate study aids.
  • Always cite the source — every artifact names the title, author, edition, and page range, with a provenance caveat.
  • Confirm authorized access — the agent only works from material you can point to a legitimate source for.
  • Flag knowledge limits — when teaching from general knowledge rather than the specific edition, it says so and tells you to verify against the source.
Net effect You get a study guide you can trust: cited, honest about its limits, and designed around active practice rather than passive summary.

How it ships

Preset skills are auto-discovered through a Go //go:embed skills directive, so adding the textbook-distillation/ directory is all that's needed — no registry or index edit. On TUI startup the bundle is extracted to ~/.lingtai-tui/utilities/ where agents read it. Frontmatter and catalog integrity are covered by the existing internal/preset and internal/tui Go tests.
